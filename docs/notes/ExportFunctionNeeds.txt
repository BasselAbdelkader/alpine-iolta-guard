  Discussion Overview

  Discussed what data should be exported from the IOLTA Guard system, in what formats, and for what purposes. Focus was on understanding compliance requirements and legal use cases.

  ---
  Export Types Considered

  Priority 1 (Essential)

  1. Client Ledger Report (PDF + Excel)
    - Status: Already partially implemented in Reports section
    - Decision: Will be covered through Reports module
  2. Trust Account Reconciliation Report (PDF)
    - Question raised: Is CSV format a legal requirement for exports?
    - Answer: No - CSV is rarely required for legal/compliance purposes

  Priority 2 (Important)

  1. Bulk CSV Export (all data for backup)
    - Question raised: What's the difference between this and backup?
    - Answer:
        - CSV Export = Readable data that can be imported to other systems
      - Database Backup = Technical file (.sql) for disaster recovery
    - Use case: Data migration, switching to different software, data portability
  2. Transaction Register by Date Range (Excel)
    - Question raised: Why do law firms need this?
    - Answer: Court/DA subpoenas, discovery requests, specific date range analysis

  Priority 3 (Nice to have)

  - Custom filtered exports
  - Scheduled/automated exports
  - Decision: Not needed at this time

  ---
  Compliance & Legal Requirements Analysis

  1. IOLTA Compliance Reports

  What's Required:
  - Three-Way Reconciliation (monthly in most states)
    - Bank statement balance
    - Book balance (system records)
    - Client ledger balance (sum of all clients)
    - All three must match

  Format Accepted:
  - PDF is most common
  - CSV acceptable if readable
  - Any format that clearly shows reconciliation

  Key Question: Do firms need to submit to state bar or just maintain for potential audit?

  ---
  2. State Bar Audits

  When They Happen:
  - Random periodic audits
  - Complaint-triggered audits
  - Discipline proceedings
  - Practice closure

  What Auditors Request:
  - Client ledgers (individual transaction history)
  - Bank statements (from actual bank)
  - Cancelled checks (proof of payments)
  - Three-way reconciliations (monthly)

  Format Preference:
  - PDF or Paper - Official audit files (most preferred)
  - Excel - Helpful for analysis
  - CSV - Acceptable but less preferred (harder to read)

  Important Note: Auditors want to see formatted reports showing proper record-keeping process, not raw data dumps.

  ---
  3. Court/DA Requests

  Scenarios:
  - Subpoenas (court orders for specific records)
  - Discovery (opposing counsel requests)
  - Criminal investigations (attorney misconduct)

  What They Request:
  - Specific client ledger
  - Specific case transactions
  - Date range transactions

  Format Accepted:
  - PDF - Preferred (official, unalterable)
  - Certified printouts - Paper with attorney signature
  - Excel - Sometimes, for analysis
  - CSV - Rarely, only if specifically requested

  Key Point: Legal requests require professional-looking reports, not raw data.

  ---
  4. Client Requests

  When:
  - End of case (final accounting)
  - Fee disputes
  - Tax purposes
  - Switching attorneys

  What They Want:
  - Their own client ledger
  - Easy to understand format

  Format Preference:
  - PDF - Professional, easy to email (most common)
  - Excel - If client wants to analyze
  - CSV - Rarely requested

  ---
  Key Findings

  PDF is the Standard for Legal/Compliance Work

  Why PDF:
  - Professional appearance
  - Cannot be easily altered
  - Printable and certifiable
  - Universally accepted by courts, auditors, clients
  - Can include firm letterhead and signatures

  CSV/Excel Use Cases:
  - Data analysis (auditors, opposing counsel)
  - Data migration (switching software)
  - Backup/portability
  - NOT for official submissions or legal documents

  ---
  Recommendations

  What Law Firms Actually Need:

  For Compliance (Monthly):
  - Three-Way Reconciliation Report (PDF)
    - Most important compliance document
    - Shows bank = books = client ledger total
    - Date-stamped, printable

  For Audits (When Requested):
  - Individual Client Ledgers (PDF)
    - Professional format with letterhead
    - Already available in Reports section

  For Legal Requests (As Needed):
  - Transaction Register (PDF or Excel)
    - Filtered by date range, client, or case
    - Printable and certifiable

  For Backup/Migration (Rarely):
  - Bulk CSV Export
    - For switching software or data portability
    - Not for compliance purposes

  ---
  Decision Made

  Export functionality will be part of the Reports section rather than a separate Settings page feature, because:
  1. Most exports need professional PDF format
  2. Reports often require signatures
  3. Use cases are primarily for official documentation
  4. Better user experience to generate reports where they're contextually relevant
